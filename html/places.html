Places to Visit

Hebron

Hebron is a city in the West Bank of enormous religious importance, and is home to the Cave of the Saints/Ibrahimi Mosque. This historic city has a diverse population and a faltering economy due to occupation restrictions

Go Hebron
Aka

Acre, also known as Akko, is a historic coastal city in northern Israel. It has a rich history, characterized by its ancient architecture, medieval fortress, and significance to various civilizations. Today, Acre is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a popular tourist destination.

Go Aka
Bethlehem

Bethlehem is a Palestinian city in the West Bank, known as the birthplace of Jesus Christ. It holds religious significance for Christians worldwide and features the Church of the Nativity, a major pilgrimage site. Bethlehem has a primarily Palestinian Arab population and is a center for cultural and religious heritage.

Go Bethlehem
Jerusalem

Jerusalem is a city in Israel and Palestine, revered by Jews, Christians, and Muslims for its religious sites, including the Western Wall and Al-Aqsa Mosque, but it's also a central point of contention in the Israeli-Palestinian conflict.

Go Jerusalem
Jericho

Jericho, in the West Bank, is an ancient Palestinian city famed for its biblical importance, including the story of the walls of Jericho. It is a destination for archaeological enthusiasts and tourists exploring its rich history. Jericho is also emblematic of the broader Israeli-Palestinian conflict.

Go Jericho
Nablus

Nablus is a Palestinian city in the West Bank known for its rich history, traditional markets, and cultural heritage. It's famous for soap and sweet production, including the renowned Nabulsi soap and desserts. Nablus has a predominantly Palestinian population and has experienced periods of conflict within the broader Israeli-Palestinian context.

Go Nablus
